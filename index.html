Frequently Asked Questions

We know you have questions, and we're ready to answer them. We're dedicated to your success, and we'll make your first physical therapy, occupational therapy and speech therapy experience comfortable and informative.

What services does your rehab center provide?

We offer Physical Therapy (PT), Occupational Therapy (OT), and Speech Therapy to help patients recover, improve function, and achieve their goals. Our services are tailored to meet individual needs, whether recovering from injury, surgery, or managing a chronic condition.

Who can benefit from your services?

Our services are for individuals of all ages who need rehabilitation due to injury, surgery, stroke, developmental delays, chronic conditions, or other health challenges. We work with patients recovering from accidents, surgeries, or those with conditions like arthritis, neurological disorders, or speech impairments.

What is Physical Therapy (PT)?

PT focuses on improving mobility, strength, balance, and function. It helps patients recover from injuries, surgeries, or conditions affecting movement, such as back pain, joint issues, or sports injuries.

Will PT be painful?

Some discomfort may occur as you work to regain strength and mobility, but your therapist will ensure exercises are safe and within your comfort level. Communication is key—let your therapist know if you experience pain.

What is Occupational Therapy (OT)?

OT helps patients regain independence in daily activities (e.g., dressing, cooking, working) after injury, illness, or disability. It focuses on improving fine motor skills, cognitive abilities, and adaptive techniques.

What does an OT session involve?

Sessions may include exercises to improve strength and coordination, training with adaptive equipment, and strategies to manage daily tasks more effectively.

What is Speech Therapy?

Speech Therapy helps patients improve communication, speech, language, and swallowing disorders. It’s beneficial for individuals with speech delays, stuttering, aphasia, or swallowing difficulties.

Who needs Speech Therapy?

Speech Therapy is for individuals with:

  • Speech or language delays (children or adults)
  • Swallowing disorders (dysphagia)
  • Aphasia or other communication issues after stroke or brain injury
  • Stuttering or voice disorders
